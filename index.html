PALESTINE HISTORY

Aain Karem Village

Mon, November 16th 2023


Ein Karem was considered one of the suburbs of Jerusalem, and a stone-paved road connected it to the main road linking Jerusalem to Jaffa, which passes three kilometers north of the village.[1][2][3] Archaeological evidence indicates that this site was inhabited since the second millennium BC. One story says that Ein Karem is the birthplace of John the Baptist. According to Christian beliefs, both Jesus and the Virgin Mary visited Ein Karem several times. There is a belief that the second Caliph, Omar bin Al-Khattab, passed by it once during the Islamic conquest, and prayed there. Ottoman records show that in the year 1596, Ain Karem was a village in the Jerusalem district (Liwa al-Quds), with a population of no more than 160 people. Ain Karem paid taxes on a number of grains and fruits.






Al-Tantoura Village

Mon, November 16th 2023


Tantoura is a Palestinian village located south of the city of Haifa, 24 km away from it and about 25 meters above sea level. The village is based on the remains of the Canaanite village (Dur), which means dwelling. Its land area is 14,520 dunums and it is surrounded by the villages of Kafr Lam, Al-Faridis, Ain Ghazal, Jisr Al-Zarqa, and Kabara. Its population in 1929 was estimated at about 750 people and in 1945 about 1,490 people.[2] Armed Zionist organizations demolished the village and displaced its people, who numbered approximately 1,728 people in 1948, on May 23, 1948. On its ruins, the Nahsholim colony was established in 1948 and the Dor colony in 1949. [3]






Mount Al-Tur

Mon, November 16th 2023


(Mount Al-Tur) or (Mount Tabor) It is the highest mountain in the lower Galilee, from the southern side of the city of Nazareth, in Marj Ibn Amer, from the north in Palestine. It is called Mount Tabor. It rises approximately 588 meters above sea level. This mountain can be seen from the Galilee, the Golan, and other Palestinian areas, especially the Jenin and Umm al-Fahm regions, as Its peak is prominent and clearly visible...









Marj Ibn Amer

Mon, November 16th 2023


It is a wide meadow between the Galilee region and the mountains of Nablus in northern historic Palestine, specifically in the northern district according to the Israeli administrative division. Its image is in the form of a triangle whose sides are: Haifa, Jenin and Tiberias. Its length is 40 km, its average width is 19 km, and its total area is 351 square km Until 1947 AD, Marj Ibn Amer was considered the bread basket of Palestine. Palestine, with its mountainous and desert terrain in addition to the valley and the small area of ​​its plains and meadows compared to the area of ​​other terrains, made Marj Ibn Amer, as it is said, one of the most beautiful plains in the world. The average number of nights in which dew forms in this meadow is about 200, which has a significant impact on agriculture in the dry summer months.
